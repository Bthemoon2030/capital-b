4OCA conversion trigger
When holders voluntarily convert their BTC-denominated debt to shares
What changing this means: A lower trigger (1.00× aggressive) means holders convert as soon as they're in-the-money. More shares get issued earlier, debt disappears from the balance sheet faster, but dilution hits harder. A higher trigger (1.50× patient) means holders sit on their BTC-denominated debt longer. Less dilution now, but if share price never reaches the trigger, the OCA goes to maturity and Capital B may have to repay holders in BTC — shrinking the stack.

Focused on the 20-month horizon (today through end of 2027) where Capital B's current capital stack is most relevant. BTC price follows the Santostasi power law per month. BTC accumulation assumed linear. Each month: cash inflows (warrants exercised when share ≥ strike × 1.30; ATM if mNAV ≥ floor and cap not exhausted; filler instruments if cash gap remains) buy BTC at that month's BTC price. Share price = (BTC stack × BTC price × mNAV) / shares outstanding, computed iteratively for convergence. OCAs convert when share ≥ conv × holder-trigger; only Moonlight (B-03) still has original gates so its holder needs share ≥ conv to convert voluntarily. Per-tranche BTC allocation per CEBE Tracker estimates.
